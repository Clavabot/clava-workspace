üåê Market Overview: The Post-BaaS Landscape

The 2023-2025 Regulatory Reset

The embedded finance industry underwent a significant transformation in 2023-2025 following OCC and FDIC enforcement actions against several sponsor banks. Key events included:

  • Blue Ridge Bank consent order (2023) - Forced exit from BaaS entirely
  • Cross River Bank enhanced oversight - Increased regulatory scrutiny
  • Synapse bankruptcy (April 2024) - $85M+ customer funds frozen, major industry wake-up call
  • CFPB 1033 Rule (Oct 2024) - Personal Financial Data Rights rule requiring secure data sharing
  • GENIUS Act provisions - New rules for direct oversight of every account and transaction
  • Fed SR 23-3 - New OASiS portal for supervisory information exchange

üî¥ Synapse Collapse ‚Äî Industry Turning Point

Synapse Financial Technologies filed for bankruptcy in April 2024, leaving ~$85M+ in customer funds frozen across multiple partner banks. Key lessons:

  • Ledger reconciliation failures ‚Äî No clear record of which bank held which customer's funds
  • Middleware risk exposed ‚Äî Synapse sat between fintechs and banks, creating opacity
  • FDIC pass-through insurance questions ‚Äî Customer protection unclear when middleware fails
  • Bank liability concerns ‚Äî Evolve Bank faced significant exposure

FINORI Opportunity: Banks are now demanding direct visibility and control. "Bank comfort" approach directly addresses post-Synapse concerns. Evidence-based compliance preparation is exactly what banks need to feel safe.

üìã Treasury Prime's Post-Synapse Response (2025)

From Treasury Prime blog analysis ‚Äî they positioned their "BankOS" as the solution to Synapse-type failures:

  • "Direct oversight of every account and transaction"
  • "From firefighting to safe, auditable embedded programs"
  • "Compliance-first strategies that build operational resilience"
  • KeyBank joined their bank network (March 2025)

Competitive Intelligence: Treasury Prime is aggressively messaging "compliance-first" post-Synapse. FINORI should differentiate on foreign fintech specialization + MT license pathway.

Sasha's existing Money Transmitter license is a strategic asset. Foreign fintechs often need MT capabilities before (or instead of) full banking partnerships.

No competitor offers this graduated pathway specifically for foreign entities.

Gap 4: Evidence-Based Compliance Preparation

Pre-Bank Compliance Readiness

Existing platforms assume fintechs are compliance-ready. FINORI can differentiate by offering:
