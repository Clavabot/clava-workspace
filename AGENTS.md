# AGENTS.md - Research Standards

## Source Requirements

- Every specific claim in the report MUST have a source (URL).
- If no source — mark as **[UNVERIFIED]** and do not draw conclusions from it.
- Prefer primary sources (company websites, press releases, LinkedIn, Crunchbase).

## Report Structure

1. **Executive Summary** — Finori positioning, market map, quick wins
2. **Competitive Landscape** — categories and clusters
3. **Competitor Table** — what they sell, to whom, bank relationship model
4. **ICP & Segments** — who is their buyer
5. **Messaging by ICP** — which pain points they sell
6. **Product Comparison** — compliance, KYB/KYC, AML/sanctions, monitoring, evidence, ledger, program mgmt, bank contracting model, GTM
7. **Pricing** (if public) and packaging
8. **Funding/Stage** (if public) and signals
9. **Risks/Weak Claims** — what was discarded or left unverified
10. **Finori Differentiation Map** — bank comfort, evidence, vendorized ops
11. **Battlecards** — 1 page per mandatory competitor (unic.co, synctera, Treasury Prime, braidfi.com)
12. **Next Actions** — what to do tomorrow, outreach angles

## Competitor Data Schema

For each competitor collect:
- URL
- Category (program manager, sponsor-bank enablement, BaaS middleware, compliance platform, ledger, onboarding)
- ICP
- Messaging by segment
- Key features
- Contracting model (who is bank counterparty, who holds compliance, who holds ledger, who does oversight)
- Evidence artifacts approach (bank-ready evidence, mapping, audit packs)
- Pricing (if public)
- Funding/stage (if public)
- Sources (URLs)

## Output Files

- `outputs/research_report.html` — 30-50 pages, structured, action-ready
- `outputs/competitors.csv` — full competitor data
- `outputs/leads.csv` — people at competitor clients
